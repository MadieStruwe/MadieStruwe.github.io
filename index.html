My Experience Outside of Computer Science

A Glimps Into My Other Studies


Minor

While taking classes at CU Boulder, I knew I needed to take a language course and some general education courses. For the language course I wanted to take something I wanted to actully learn, as a result I took Swedish. During my two years of Swedish classes I came to appreciate the Swedish culture and history. As a result, I decided to take "Scandinavian" classes for my general education courses. After looking at the requirments for a Nordic Studies Minor, I discovred that I have already taken most of the classes I needed. As such, I decided to get a Nordic Studies minor.

To learn more about what classes I took and what they entailed, you can click here.

Writing Classes

Even as a Computer Science Major, written and verbal communication is still extremly important. I have taken a few writing courses while attending CU. I have taken a couple official "Writing" courses at CU. One class was about comic books and graphic novels, and to how those are valid forms of books and not as just a means of simple entertainment. Another class I took allowed me to write a scientific paper of my own choosing. I decided to write a refutation against Nick Bostrom as to why AI is nothing to fear. The final course I took as not techniclly Writing course, but rather a "History" course.

The final course I took as not techniclly Writing course, but rather a "History" course. This class was "Vennince and Florence During The Renaissance." I learned much about Italy during the Renaissance, but students also focused on writing a paper of their choosing about something from the Italian Reniacance. I was partial to the idea of colors in Reniacance paintings, and as a result I wrote my paper of the colors used in Reniacance paintings and the effects they have on the viewer.
